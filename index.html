About Me

Hey, thanks for visiting. I'm a PhD Candidate in this department, at this university researching batteries in this lab led by my Principal Investigator, Dr. Daniel Steingart.

Before joining Princeton, I received my BS and MS in this department, at this university in 2016.

After my undergraduate, I jumped briefly into industry, working as a utilities and smart grid technology consultant for Accenture and as a battery scientist at a cleantech startup. Following, I decided to pursue a PhD researching lithium metal batteries. Broadly, I think about energy, and specifically, how to store more of it in a more efficient and safe manner.

Research Interests

My previous work has focused on carbon nanotube applications and silicon-graphite anodes for lithium ion batteries. My current research topic is focused on understanding how to electrochemically synthesize uniform lithium metal for next-gen batteries
